Xylella advances in the Piana degli Ulivi: also found on rosemary and myrtle
Xylella fastidiosa is advancing in Puglia: 38 infected plants have been identified in an area between the north of the province of Brindisi and the south of the province of Bari . An outbreak was also detected in a hitherto undamaged area around Polignano a Mare . The bacterium was also found in two rosemary plants and one myrtle plant. The reports come from the institutional website “Emergenza Xylella” from which we learn that of the 38 infected plants, 16 are in the countryside of Polignano a Mare, six in the countryside of Monopoli and 16 in the countryside of Fasano . Of the 16 infected plants in the countryside of Polignano , 9 are part of the new outbreak in an undamaged area, in the countryside that runs along the provincial road 121 Conversano-Polignano , in an area about two kilometers from the town, highly frequented by vehicles and heavy vehicles . Xylella fastidiosa is a bacterium that causes the complete desiccation of olive trees. It is estimated that so far, the pathogen has affected about 21 million olive trees in Puglia, since the first outbreak was identified in 2013, in the “Li Sauli” district, in the countryside of Gallipoli . "We have always maintained that Xylella fastidiosa would not have waited for the times of bureaucracy, politics and justice - recalls the president of the CIA (Italian Farmers Confederation), Gennaro Sicolo - we were not wrong, given the progress of the bacterium which has now reached province of Bari. On Xylella it is now necessary to implement the tools and use the funds obtained in years of battles, to redesign the economic and productive future of the compromised provinces, implementing replanting even with species other than the olive tree, and overgrafting, the latter for save the plain of monumental olive trees, allocating, overcoming the ever-present bureaucratic obstacles, using the compensation allocated, with a more incisive preventive action through monitoring, eradications and the implementation of good agricultural practices ". Gennaro Sicolo calls for the appointment of an extraordinary commissioner for the killing of infected plants with extraordinary powers and tools.
