DNA at the weak points of the flavescence dorée of the vine
Agroscope researchers have succeeded in deciphering the genetic heritage of flavescence dorée. The objective now is to find weak points
ike in France, flavescence dorée is a real scourge in Switzerland, where it first appeared in 2004, in the canton of Ticino. “The affected vines must be removed and destroyed. If infection occurs, steps are taken to control the species of leafhopper that is spreading the disease. Alternatives to the use of insecticides are being sought,” recalls Agroscope de Changins, which, in a press release, announces that it has succeeded in deciphering the genetic heritage of the pathogen. The disease is caused by phytoplasmas, bacteria that lack a cell wall. To be able to eradicate this disease, it is important to understand how the pathogen spreads and what happens to the affected plant. This is why Agroscope researchers have deciphered the genetic heritage of the pathogen Candidatus phytoplasma vitis . “Unlike other phytoplasmas, the structure of the genetic heritage of this pathogen is stable. This is an essential discovery, because a stable genetic heritage means that weak points also behave in a stable way. It is therefore easier to find them and use them to eliminate the pathogen,” they say. They will now be able to study the function of genes revealed by genome sequencing and understand how the pathogen bypasses a plant's defenses to make it sick.
