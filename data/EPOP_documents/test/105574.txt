Xylella, new outbreak in the Bari area: 18 infected olive trees in Polignano
The state of natural disaster was recognized in Lecce, Brindisi and Taranto
In Puglia and Basilicata The case September 18, 2022 Online editing A new Xylella outbreak has been identified in Polignano a Mare, in the Bari area, with 18 infected olive trees. This was supported by Coldiretti Puglia, who raised the alarm. "The state of natural disaster in Lecce, Brindisi and Taranto was recognized - says the organization of the agricultural sector, but the advance of the disease in the province of Bari with the new outbreak in Polignano a Mare and the 18 infected olive trees found in free zone imposes a more incisive containment strategy and the identification of new resources to deal with damage to the olive-growing heritage, to work and to tourism that has reached almost 3 billion euros. "The Ministry of Agriculture has recognized the state of calamities from January to December 2021 caused by Xylella fastidiosa for the provinces of Lecce and Brindisi and for the municipalities of the province of Taranto Avetrana, Carosino, Faggiano, Fragagnano, Lizzano, Manduria, Maruggio, Monteiasi, Pulsano, Roccaforzata, San Marzano di San Giuseppe, Sava, Torricella.
