New detections of Xylella fastidiosa lead to update of demarcated areas The Directorate General for Food and Veterinary Medicine (DGAV) published Order No. 64/G/2022, of September 26, which determines the establishment of a new Demarcated Zone for the quarantine bacteria Xylella fastidiosa in the parish of Colares, in the municipality of Sintra. This decision comes after obtaining a positive result for X. fastidiosa in this parish, following the prospecting work carried out by the official services - complying with European Union regulations on protective measures against plant pests, phytosanitary measures to prevent the introduction and dispersion in the EU territory of bacteria, as well as phytosanitary protection measures aimed at eradicating X. fastidious. According to the DGAV, «the presence of the bacterium Xylella fastidiosa was laboratory confirmed in a sample of Lavandula dentata, collected in the parish of Colares, municipality of Sintra», and «the subspecies of the bacterium has not yet been identified». The DGAV adds that «the plants identified infected, to date, in the demarcated area belong to the following species: Lavandula dentata». As a result, it is determined – through Order No. 64/G/2022, which you can consult here – the establishment of a demarcated area for this bacterium and the measures that must be applied for its eradication. By the way, remember that the DGAV had published Dispatch No. 62/G/2022, of 19 September, which updates the Demarcated Zone of the Metropolitan Area of Porto to X. fastidiosa. This decision arose following the prospecting work carried out by the official services in that demarcated area, in which the presence of the bacterium was confirmed in nine new locations, in the municipalities of Vila Nova de Gaia, Santa Maria da Feira, Porto and Espinho. According to Dispatch N.º 62/G/2022, which you can consult here, “the identification of the subspecies of the bacteria in these new samples is still in the confirmation process”. The infected plants identified in the demarcated area, which amount to around 60 to date, belong to multiple genera and species – detailed in the Order –, including citrus.
