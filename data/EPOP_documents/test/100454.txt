A Japanese beetle captured in Freiburg A male Japanese beetle, a highly invasive species, monitored by the German authorities, was discovered in a trap set at Freiburg-en-Brisgau station. A male Japanese beetle (popillia japonica) was recently captured in a pheromone trap installed at Freiburg im Breisgau station. In the fall of 2021, a first alert took place in the south of Baden Württemberg, as well as in Alsace. Considered to be highly invasive, this species observed in Freiburg could push European authorities to decide on quarantine measures in already colonized areas. Renowned for its voracity, this insect represents a threat to 300 plant species: from tomatoes to oaks. Originally from Asia, the Japanese beetle was already present in southern Europe, particularly in Italy. Its arrival in northern Europe is mainly due to transport. The species travels in freight cars. This is why, last year, the German authorities had placed traps in the stations of Baden Württemberg. 42 specimens have since been captured there in less than a year.
