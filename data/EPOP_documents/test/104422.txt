AGRICULTURE: Map registers 50 pesticides; half are organic products - ACSURS
Porto Alegre, August 31, 2022 – Act number 38 of the Department of Plant Health and Agricultural Inputs of the Agricultural Defense Department, published this Tuesday (30) in the Diário Oficial da União, brings the record of 50 formulated agricultural pesticides, that is, products that will effectively be available for use by farmers. Of these, 25 are products biological products, nine of which are approved for use in organic agriculture. With the registration of these products today, there are already 69 low toxicity products registered in 2022. Low-impact products are important for agriculture not only because of the toxicological and environmental aspects, but also for benefiting phytosanitary support crops insufficient, as these products are approved by target pests and can be recommended in any culture. Among the novelties of biological products, the fungus Cordyceps javanica, an alternative to the management of resistant populations of whitefly, won its first record on the Mapa, using also approved for organic farming. Entomopathogenic fungi are among the most important natural enemies of whiteflies and penetrate directly into the insect, without the need to of ingestion. Another biological controller, the nematode Heterorhabditis bacteriophora, which held only a product on the market, has now received authorization for use in a caterpillar control product Spodoptera frugiperda and Scaptocoris castanea. Spodoptera has recently been considered as a priority pest for agriculture. Also for Spodoptera frugiperda there is the first record of the entomopathogenic nematode Steinernema carpocapsae, which in addition to controlling the Spodoptera caterpillar is also indicated for the control of fungus flies (Bradysia matogrossensis) and sugarcane weevil (Sphenophorus levis). For organic agriculture, nine products were offered, including the parasitoid Tetrastichus howardi, the parasitoid wasp Telenomus podisi and the fungi Beauveria bassiana and Metarhizium anisopliae, increasing the range of options for the farmer. Regarding registered chemical products, for the first time a product formulated active ingredient base Espiropidion will be offered to farmers. It is an insecticide which has been under review since 2018 and is recommended for aphid, thrips and whitefly control in cotton, beans, soy and tomato crops. The information is from the Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Supply (Map).
