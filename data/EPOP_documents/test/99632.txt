Banana Moko
(Ralstonia solanacearum Race 2)
In Mexico, there is an established area of just over 80 thousand 500 hectares, distributed in 16 States, with an average yield of 31 tons/ha. The states of Chiapas and Tabasco take the lead within the South-Southeast region and together contribute 53% of the volume of national production (Atlas Agroalimentario, 2020), which makes it the third most important product within the group of the fruits. 9 varieties are cultivated: Enano Gigante (or Gran Enano), Macho Dominico, Valery, Pera, Tabasco, Morado, Manzano and Thai Banana; whose value amounts to more than 9,409 million pesos (SIAP, 2020), permanently generating a large number of direct and indirect jobs in the producing areas. On an international scale, the country is positioned in 12th place, contributing 2.4 million tons that are exported to 30 commercial destinations, where the United States is the main importer. The bacterium , commonly known as Ralstonia solanacearum Race 2 "Moko del Plátano" is a phytopathogen that attacks all varieties of bananas, also considered one of the most serious phytosanitary problems affecting Musaceae in tropical and subtropical regions of the world, as well as being a determining factor in the restriction production business. This disease, in some countries of Latin America and the Caribbean, the disease caused by R. solanacearum affects cooking bananas of the "Bluggoe" subgroup (ABB), and dessert bananas belonging to the Cavendish group (AAA), as well as belonging to the Heliconiaceae family. The importance of the pest lies in its permanence during all stages of crop development, since there is no cure, once established, it is essential to establish biosecurity measures and activities that support risk mitigation. Likewise, in the field, the expression of R. solanacearum Race 2 is difficult since it is often confused with phytosanitary problems such as the presence of other diseases, wilting due to water stress, nutrient deficiency, misdirected chemical applications, among others; present causes often take time away from safe containment and timely management. The conditions that favor its development and survival are: a) conditions related to the environment; soil, water, air and insects, b) plant material; movement of diseased and asymptomatic corms and suckers and c) inefficient phytosanitary measures, which are a pathway that contributes to the efficient spread of the pest. However, the most important contamination is through the movement of infected plants . The implementation of phytosanitary measures carried out by technical personnel of the State Plant Health Committees in coordination with the producers, has allowed the pest to remain confined to 10 Municipalities of Chiapas and Tabasco, fully identified and under official control; in compliance with the operational strategy and the provisions of NOM-068-SAG/FITO-2015 , which establishes phytosanitary measures to combat banana moko and prevent its dispersion. The rest of the producing states are served through the Phytosanitary Epidemiological Surveillance Program . Additionally, the Campaign against Moko del Plátano contributes with actions to reinforce the early detection of the fungus “Fusariosis de las Musáceas” , commonly known as “Foc R4T” (Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cubense Tropical Race 4) a pest that is absent in Mexico .
