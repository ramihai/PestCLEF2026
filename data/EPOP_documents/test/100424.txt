“Reborn from Xylella”: among the nine projects in the final also two from Brindisi
Initiative launched by Banca Sella and the University of Salento, with the patronage of the Puglia Region and in partnership with the innovation platform for startups and companies Sellalab and Dpixel
“Reborn from Xylella”: among the nine projects in the final also two from Brindisi Initiative launched by Banca Sella and the University of Salento, with the patronage of the Puglia Region and in partnership with the innovation platform for startups and companies Sellalab and Dpixel BRINDISI - There are also two Brindisi projects among the nine admitted to the final phase of the call for ideas "Rinascere dalla Xylella", an initiative launched by Banca Sella and the University of Salento, with the patronage of the Puglia Region and in partnership with the platform of innovation for startups and companies Sellalab and dpixel, with the aim of identifying and developing innovative ideas and projects for the revival of the Apulian economy and territory through solutions capable of countering and reducing the contagion from Xylella, regenerating the affected lands. These are "Xfarms" and "Vax". XFarms the SME of San Vito dei Normanni intervenes on the territorial impact. The project aims to reforest the affected land with an agri-forest made up of trees, perennials and other species. Self-sufficiency is guaranteed through the sale of products and the enhancement of the space. Vax: the key idea of the Brindisi team is to manipulate the behavior of Philaenus Spumarius (Sputacchina), vector insect of Xylella, by inducing a reduction of couplings through a technology with vibrations. Of the 40 projects presented, 36 come from Italy (29 in particular from Puglia) while 4 applications came from abroad (Spain, Portugal, Egypt and South Africa). A committee composed of experts from the University of Salento, Banca Sella, Sellalab and dpixel has admitted to the final phase 9 innovative projects concerning, in particular, reforestation, the fight against Xylella vector insects, the use of microorganisms to limit the parasite, biodiversity, social services, the use of immuno-sensors and the spread of sustainable olive growing. These are the other finalists: Adopt Me - The startup from Barletta promotes a model of adoption of crops and olive trees in the territories affected by Xylella for the support of producers and the absorption of CO2. Azienda Agricola Melcarne - The Lecce project proposes the selection and enhancement of new olive cultivars characterized by resistance and adaptability to innovative olive growing systems. ABZ Group - The Ferrara team works on monoculture and drought. The aim of the project is to increase and enhance rural biodiversity in terms of vegetation to ensure greater resistance against future similar events. Mirtiko! - The Lecce team's project aims to restore the Mediterranean scrub to provide social services (apitherapy with hives, trekking etc.), economic services (production of myrtle and pistachio liqueurs) and ecosystem services (CO2 absorption, pollinators, biological diversity). Olivami - The startup from Martano (Lecce) promotes post-Xylella reforestation through an innovative model of sustainable and participatory olive growing, which allows anyone to remotely adopt one or more Salento olive trees. S2G Technologies - The Roman startup offers a reading kit capable of indicating the presence of the bacterium even in the initial stages thanks to the use of inexpensive and immediate response immuno-sensors. SIRITA - The activities of the Campobasso team concern the selection, evaluation and development of new formulations based on natural substances and antagonistic microorganisms with antimicrobial activity against Xylella. The 9 finalist projects will benefit from an accompanying phase to perfect the business model which will be followed by the "matching" of the solutions developed with local companies. The “Demo-Day” is scheduled for the end of October with the awarding of the 3 winning projects for each category identified (researchers and university students, startups and entrepreneurial teams and SMEs). Each project will win a 10 thousand euro prize offered by Banca Sella. The winning companies will also have the opportunity to access the dpixel Startup Incubation System, the opportunity to work in one of the Sellalab hubs in Italy, to use the laboratories and equipment of the University of Salento, to receive free advice provided by Innovation Financer by dpixel and the possibility of integrating its solution within the offer of local companies and partners participating in Call4Ideas.
