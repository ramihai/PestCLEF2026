To prevent health risks, they destroyed 62 citrus plants that were sold on the Artigas Highway - News - Elentrerios.com
The National Agrifood Health and Quality Service (Senasa), together with the National Seed Institute (INASE) and the Gendarmer... The citrus propagation material was detected during a routine inspection by technicians from the Entre Ríos Regional Center of Senasa, carried out a few days ago in the traditional sales stalls that are located next to the shoulder of National Route No. 14 José Gervasio Artigas Highway.
There, according to the control agency, the existence of citrus plants for street sales without the corresponding health documentation, that is, the authorization in the National Phytosanitary Registry for the Qualification of Nurseries (Renfo), as reseller nurseries, and the sanitary documentation that guarantees their origin.
The Senasa agents notified the stallholders that the situation generated a high sanitary risk in relation to the pest declared as quarantine in Law 26,888, (the bacteria Candidatus Liberibacter spp), the causative agent of Huanglongbing and which constitutes a violation of article 1 of the resolutions ones Senasa No. 930/09 and 1678/2019; therefore,
ordered the confiscation and destruction of the citrus material to prevent health risks.
Warning
Senasa reminded buyers of citrus plants that they should only purchase material produced under cover, as established in its Resolution 930/09 and of certified quality in accordance with the provisions of Resolution 149/98 of the former Secretary of Agriculture, Livestock, Fisheries and Food (SAGPyA) and its amendments, whose transport must be accompanied by the electronic Plant Transit Document (DTV-e) that must be kept for two years, as proof of the origin of the planted material as established by Senasa Resolution 27/2019.
HLB is the most destructive pest of citrus fruits worldwide, which so far has no cure, therefore it is of the utmost importance to avoid its spread, complying with the regulations in force, they warned from the agency.
The Senas He also recalled that it is mandatory to report any suspicion of the presence of the pest by contacting the toll-free telephone line: 0800-999-2386 or by email: alertahlb@senasa.gob.ar
