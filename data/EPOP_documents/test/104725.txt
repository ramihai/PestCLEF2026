The Balearic Islands, an example in the European field in the containment of Xylella fastidiosa
The Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food has received a delegation of Plant Health technicians from the Administration of the island of Corsica who are visiting the Balearic Islands to learn about the management of the containment of the Xylella fastidiosa plague and
As explained by the Minister of Agriculture, Mae de la Concha, "this delegation returns the visit we made to them in October 2019, where the highly advanced level of research that we have in the Balearic Islands in this matter was demonstrated". "They want to know everything we have done here, with experimental fields, in research on more resistant species and in the fight against the vector, among others, to coexist with the bacteria that we have, unfortunately, installed in the Balearic Islands, and that does not have to prevent them from continuing with agriculture", he explained. De la Concha also recalled that aid has been launched to alleviate the uprooting of trees that has occurred in the Balearic Islands and that will allow the planting of almost 15,000 almond trees this year. Since the detection of the bacteria on the Islands in October 2016, Agriculture has launched research projects on the varietal resistance of the most affected crops on the Islands, the types of vector insects, the characterization of the bacteria and of the subspecies present in the Islands: multiplex , fastidiosa and pauca (present only in Ibiza). The budget allocated annually to Xylella fastidiosa from the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food is approximately 1.3 million euros, in addition to successive research projects on the subject. Thus, for example, 1.05 million euros have been allocated to "Design and implementation of control strategies against Xylella fastidiosa" , financed with funds from the Sustainable Tourism Tax (ITS), and made up of six different research lots ; 150,000 euros from a project of the National Institute for Agricultural and Food Research and Technology (INIA); 240,000 euros from a recent European project to be developed in coordination with the Institute for Sustainable Agriculture (IAS-CSIC); or the first aid of the dried fruit restructuring plan, of 2.3 million euros, for the planting and replanting of almond and carob trees. With this first aid, 20 new hectares of almond trees will be planted, one of the crops most affected by this bacterium, which will mean 14,862 trees. So far, 1,360 positive plants have been detected in 37 different host species from a sample of 18,016 plants. The positives have been registered in Mallorca, Menorca and Ibiza. Formentera is the only island, at the moment, free of Xylella fastidiosa .
