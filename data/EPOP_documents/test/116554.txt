Fruit fly control also involves individuals
LITTLE ISLAND. Agricultural production is not just a farmer's business. At the heart of its three-hectare citrus orchard in the upper... "There are plenty of bugs, mites, mealybugs, flies that attack. As we are in sustainable cultivation, we only use approved products. Maintenance is done every day of the year, it is a bit like a baby", begins the farmer who planted his first citrus fruit on his land 47 years ago. "There are so many diseases, with fruit flies and the arrival of the greening virus, it affects us", laments the Petite-Îlelois. This Tuesday, he welcomed to his orchard a delegation made up of agents from the Department, the Fdgdon (Departmental Federation of defense groups against harmful organisms) and the Daaf (Direction of food, agriculture and the forest) for a sanitary technical day around the fight against the fruit fly, a real scourge for crops in Reunion. "THE FIGHT MUST GO FURTHER" The fruit fly, from its name Bactrocera dorsalis, is a plague originating in Asia which is now present throughout the Indian Ocean basin. Arrived on our island between 2017 and 2019, it has already done a lot of damage. In 2018, the orchards recorded 100% losses in Saint-Gilles. Losses gradually lowered to 50% then 40% thanks to the collective actions of the Fdgdon, supported by the Department. Rotting of fruit tissue, coulures, secondary diseases, emergence of maggots, mangoes but also bananas, pitayas, pineapple, tomatoes, watermelons, pumpkins and citrus fruits are attacked by this fly. > Since then, the actions have multiplied. "The fight against the fruit fly must go further and it is for this reason that we launched, during 2020, calls for projects to launch an experiment on alternative solutions in the fields, but also for the fight against conservation. ...
