Landesanstalt für Landwirtschaft und Horticulture (LLG) informs about new finds of the Asian longhorned beetle (ALB) in the quarantine zone in Magdeburg
The press service for active press work: sending reports and media to editors, subscribers and readers. September 8th, 2022 - state capital Magdeburg State Institute for Agriculture and Horticulture (LLG) provides information about new finds of the Asian longhorned beetle (ALB) in the quarantine zone in Magdeburg During the monitoring work to monitor the Asian longhorned beetle (ALB) in Magdeburg on August 22, 2022, a female ALB was again found in a pheromone trap in the Wiesenpark, in the north-eastern area of the quarantine zone set up for this purpose. The trap location is close to the last ALB trap catches in this area from 2018 and 2020 and also the 2016 felling zones. After the trap was caught, the LLG and the monitoring staff of the city gardens and cemeteries of the state capital Magdeburg immediately carried out additional intensive monitoring measures in the area concerned. On August 23, 2022, a pasture with typical ALB symptoms was found. Upon closer examination, an ALB-typical larva was found in a branch and immediately transferred to the National Reference Laboratory of the Julius Kühn Institute in Braunschweig (JKI) for PCR analysis. The laboratory result confirmed the suspicion. In the days that followed, other trees suspected of being infested were identified by employees in so-called ground monitoring, rope climbers and also by using a drone from the JKI. Larvae were also discovered on these trees, which can be clearly assigned to the species ALB based on their morphological characteristics. This was confirmed by further laboratory tests at the JKI. Due to the weather and the time of year, the potential flight phase of the ALB is still ongoing. There is imminent danger because it cannot be ruled out it is possible that more beetles will burrow out and the species will spread further. In accordance with the EU implementation decision, the LLG therefore ordered the immediate felling of the infested trees and other potential host trees in the immediate vicinity. The felling work will be completed by the middle of the week. The wood produced is shredded on site and transported in sealed containers for thermal utilization under the supervision of the LLG. At the same time, the LLG will now set up a felling zone with a radius of 100 m around the infested trees, in which all specified host plants must then be felled and destroyed promptly. The aim of the measures is to eradicate the pest in the area. This is the only way to prevent even greater damage. The LLG suspects another infestation in the northern area of Lake Neustadt. A trap catch was recorded here in 2021, but the origin has not yet been found. Both here and in the Wiesenpark, the monitoring is now being carried out particularly intensively. According to the EU implementation decision, the LLG is obliged to eradicate the quarantine pest. Significantly fewer finds have been registered in recent years, which means that the problem areas have been narrowed down more and more. In order to be able to work even more effectively in areas that are difficult to access, the short-term use of tracking dogs, which specialize in tracking down the beetle and its larvae, is currently being examined. The mission could still take place in September. The fellings of the last few days were accompanied by a number of scientists from the JKI, various colleges and universities and an ALB expert from the Swiss Plant Protection Service. Citizen reports are also particularly important. LLG has had very good experiences here. There is also a reporting requirement. Every suspicious activity report is checked on site by LLG's ALB specialists. The ALB hotline can still be reached on 03471-334-253 or via alb@llg.mule.sachsen-anhalt.de. Due to the current infestation, the quarantine zone, which currently covers 49 km², must be maintained for another four years until 2026. The intensive monitoring work on site will continue for at least as long. The general decree of the LLG will be revised according to the current finds by the end of the year at the latest. Whether there will be major changes in the expansion of the quarantine zone is not yet foreseeable.
