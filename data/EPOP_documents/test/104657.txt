The Asian longhorned beetle (ALB) is up to mischief in Zell in the Lucerne hinterland. The particularly dangerous pest kills trees within a few years. So far it has only been reported four times in Switzerland - and each time it has been successfully eradicated. Attentive residents noticed individual specimens of the beetle in Zell, as the Lucerne Department of the Environment announced on Tuesday. The ALB is considered one of the most dangerous hardwood pests worldwide and is notifiable in Switzerland. The beetle is 2.5 to 3.5 centimeters long, shiny black with irregular light spots on the elytra. It attacks deciduous trees such as maple, poplar, willow, horse chestnut, birch, plane tree, beech and other hardwood species. The larvae eat their way through the bast and tree, damaging the ducts and killing the tree. No natural enemies in Europe Because it has no natural enemies in Europe, the beetle must be fought and eradicated, the authorities said. Due to global trade flows, it usually reaches Switzerland in packaging wood from East Asia. In this country, the Asian longhorned beetle appeared for the first time in 2011. It was last eradicated in the pest-ridden canton of Friborg in 2019. The beetle is considered to be sluggish and usually needs two years for its development in the wood. For this reason, there is "justified hope" in Lucerne that the problem will be mastered. Search teams with climbing specialists and sniffer dogs are now deployed in Zell to search the affected area for beetles. Host trees must be felled and chopped up. The economic and ecological damage to the affected areas is high. The canton is setting up a special collection point for green waste for the people of Zell.
