Xylella, 30 infected olive trees in Monopoli and 9 in Polignano
At the third monitoring started in May 2022. In less than two months 59 diseased plants were found
In Puglia and Basilicata Monitoring An olive tree felled due to xylella fastidiosa July 14, 2022 Online editing There are 30 olive trees infected by Xylella in Monopoli and 9 in Polignano a Mare, at the third monitoring started in May 2022. In less than two months 59 diseased trees have been found. This is what Coldiretti Puglia reports, based on Infoxylella data, following the update of the dashboard of the 'Emergency Xylellà' institutional website of the Puglia Region. 31 diseased plants are found in the buffer zone - specified by Coldiretti - in three outbreaks already reported, plus the presence of a new one in Monopoli. In Polignano, then, 8 infected olive trees, with the check not yet completed. "On the basis of what emerges from the statistical analysis conducted by the research institutions - reads a note from Coldiretti Puglia - a responsible assessment must be opened regarding the provisions of the EU regulation approved on August 14, 2020". "Regulation - he continues - which has reduced at 50 meters, from the 100 meters initially envisaged, the buffer area or the radius of the outbreak area around the plants found infected and subject to mandatory cutting to remove them from the spreading action of vector insects, such as the spitting leafhopper "." If the spittoon walks up to 400 meters in a season, the buffer area of 50 meters - according to Coldiretti - is decidedly insufficient to contain the risk of contagion, so you only get an action to slow down the spread, certainly not the extinction of the more advanced outbreaks in buffer areas on the epidemic front which would require much more drastic actions ». "The spread of Xylella Fastidiosa - concludes the note - could cost billions of euros in the next 50 years in Europe, while in Italy, if the expansion of the infected area is not stopped, the economic impact could grow up to 5.2 billion. euro - based on the study of the prestigious American journal PNAS (Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America) on the evaluation of the impact of Xylella fastidiosa pauca on olive growing in Italy, Greece and Spain "
