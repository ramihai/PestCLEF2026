INTA SPACE September 12, 2022 They reduce the presence of the insect that transmits HLB by 21% These are preliminary results obtained in demonstration lots located in family farms in Argentina, Uruguay and Paraguay. During the study – carried out by specialists from INTA and other institutions – they also recorded a 60% increase in natural enemies. The trial was carried out within the framework of a project co-financed by Fontagro. HLB –Huanglongbing– is the most destructive disease of citrus plantations in the world, caused by the bacterium Candidatus Liberibacter spp . The spread of the disease responds to the movement of diseased plant material and the presence of the vector Diaphorina citri, as a spreading agent. Since there is no cure, prevention is based on the use of healthy material, constant monitoring of the crop and the vector insect, its control, and early identification of the infected plant for its elimination. The disease is present in much of the American continent. It has been registered in Argentina since 2012 and in Paraguay since 2013. For this reason, and due to the existence of more than 226 hectares with citrus and more than 6,000 family citrus growers in the region's citrus chain, a team of researchers and extensionists from various institutions promotes the implementation of Integrated Pest and Disease Management (IPM), over the routine application of non-selective agrochemicals that generate risks and damage to the environment. The work is part of the project co-financed by the Regional Fund for Agricultural Technology (Fontagro) "Sustainable control of the HLB insect vector in family citrus farming in Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay and Bolivia." The results were presented in the framework of the virtual seminar "Experiences on the control and eradication of the citrus disease HLB in Latin America and the Caribbean" organized by the Inter-American Institute for Cooperation on Agriculture (IICA) Venezuela. During the meeting, advances in the prevention and control of HLB in Latin America and the Caribbean were presented, with the participation of specialists from Mexico, Venezuela, Argentina, and Brazil, who favored the exchange of information, the panorama of prevention, and the approach of the problem, and the challenges currently facing the region. From Argentina, Silvana Giancola, leader of the Fontagro Project, presented the results of Integrated Pest Management (IPM) in demonstration and conventional plots located in family citrus farms in three countries, Argentina, Uruguay and Paraguay. In this sense and, "based on the periodic monitoring carried out in these lots, a 21% decrease in individuals of Diaphorina citri -vector of HLB- and a 60% increase in natural enemies was evidenced," said Giancola. In addition, Giancola explained other findings that show that IPM would reduce direct production costs, based on the timely control of pests and diseases and the use of more environmentally friendly products, positively impacting the quality of the harvested fruit. These results were obtained after working with comparative data in two IPM implementation campaigns (2020 and 2021) in a network of 13 pairs of lots located in family citrus farms in the respective countries. Each pair of plots or project site has a demonstrator plot (LD) –with IPM– and a conventional plot (LC) –with traditional management– with a similar area, the same species and citrus variety. “The monitoring of the HLB vector and natural enemies is carried out periodically –every 15 days or once a month, depending on the time of year– through the application of 3 methods: visual observation of outbreaks, adhesive cards and tapping of branches, which allows to improve the possibilities of detection”, expressed Giancola. The first two experiences of mass release of the Tamarixi radiata wasp –specific parasitoid of the HLB vector– were also carried out in project lots in commercial lots in the region, from the massive rearing of this natural enemy, which is carried out in the INTA Agricultural Experimental Station in Bella Vista –Corrientes, Argentina–, within the framework of the agreement: INTA-FONDAGRO, and in the National Institute of Agrarian Innovation (INIA) Salto –Uruguay–, within the framework of the agreement: INIA-UPEFRUY- MGAP. This international project has the participation of more than one hundred researchers and extensionists from the following institutions: INTA and Fundación ArgenINTA as executing agency; INIA –Uruguay–, National University of Itapuá/University Foundation of Itapúa –Paraguay– and Autonomous Municipal Government of Bermejo –Bolivia–, as co-executing agencies; the National Service for Agrifood Health and Quality (Senasa) and the Argentine Citrus Federation (Federcitrus), both from Argentina, and the Union of Fruit Producers and Exporters of Uruguay (Upefruy), as associated bodies. The study began in 2019 and will run until the beginning of 2024. The objective is to adapt, disseminate and raise awareness of the Integrated Pest Management technology in the control of the citrus HLB vector in family citrus farming in the countries involved, for which the Lots function as a territorial unit for the scaling of sustainable management of the vector and prevention of HLB.
